Bike Share Frame Redesign

This project used Ansys finite element analysis to evaluate an aluminum bike frame and redesign it for bike share usability. I removed the top tube and reshaped the down tube into a lower step-through geometry so riders could mount the bike more easily.

The original 6061 aluminum frame had a maximum deformation of 0.112 mm and a maximum equivalent stress of 42.38 MPa, giving a factor of safety of 6.12. The redesigned frame improved usability but raised maximum deflection to 1.12 mm and stress to 136.29 MPa, with an initial minimum factor of safety of 1.902.

To bring the design back above the safety target, I performed parametric optimization around the bottom bracket shell. Increasing the local tube thickness to 4 mm raised the minimum factor of safety above 2, and direct FEA verified the response surface prediction.

Technologies used: Ansys, finite element analysis, shell modeling, parametric optimization
