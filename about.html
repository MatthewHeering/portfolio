About

Building mechanical systems that are thoughtful, testable, and useful.

I am a mechanical engineering student at Cornell University with a passion for designing and building mechanical systems. Through coursework and projects, I have developed skills in CAD design, finite element analysis, control systems, robotics, and technical communication.

I enjoy solving engineering problems that ask for both analytical thinking and practical judgment. My work spans fluid mechanics analysis, mechatronics design, system dynamics, and mechanical design projects that move from sketches and models into physical prototypes.

I am especially drawn to projects where the final result has to survive contact with the real world: parts need to fit, sensors need to behave, reports need to explain the reasoning, and teams need to make good tradeoffs under pressure.
